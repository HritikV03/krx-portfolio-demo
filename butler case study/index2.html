BUTLER

Butler is a location based task reminder app. Butler goes beyond the limitations of time and date for completing a task; get a hassle free experience and get rid of those irritating alarms which are only based on date and time, instead get reminded only when you’ll reach near that particular location.

The Problems

1: Creating “set location” feature by providing option for current or manual location but it must be independent i.e free from any date and time constraints.

2: Counter challenge 1; what if user’s task for a particular location is based on date and time.

3: Interface should be soothing and self explainatory to enhance user experience and engagement.

My Role

As this project is carried out under freelance, I led the UX/UI domain which includes research and conceptualization, user insight analysis, user task flow, wireframing and UI. Furthermore, several interaction sessions were conducted with development team to analyse the architecture.

Research and Analysis

We tend to underestimate the importance of reducing the learning curve when users experience products for the first time. However, creating familiarity can be helpful to make users ready when they experience our product for the first time. Knowing where to find key actions, using standardized gestures, and structuring content in a way that is familiar to consume gives them the feeling of control and safety to navigate with confidence and manage issues by themselves.
As a sweet fruit of our research, we found out that simply removing date and time constraints and basing the whole mechanism of app entirely on location feature is going to limit the user engagement, so instead we came up with the solution of introducing choice feature. Precisely, we will ask from user itself about its choice.

User Interview

In order to carry out the user empathy approach, user interview plays a vital role in understanding the thought process of user. For this project I’d interviewed 5 candidates, as a result we were able to collect enough data to analyse the problem architecture and improve the whole experience and also in validating the researched data.

User Insights

User interviews led us to the following insights:

1: Only having location based reminder option is not a go to option, beacuse as it is a entirely situation based pespective.

2: Contrary of insight 1, having only location based reminding feature will completely neglect the time and date constraint, which is also a situation based perspective what is user wants to get a reminder on a particular date and time at particular location respectively.

Challenge and Solution Analogy

Analogy 1: Aman wants to buy a pack of domestic items from the ‘new lane store’ on his way home. New lane store is in between his way to home and office. So, he wants to be reminded to buy items on his way back for which he simply put a location reminder by choosn “only location” mode of Butler and he’ll be reminded whenever he reaches that location, irrespective of time and date constraints.

Analogy 2: Aman’s wedding anniversary is on June 10, he is planning a date for his wife; however, beacuse of hectic schedule and rush hours at office, there’s a good chance that he may forget to buy flowers and gifts for his wife. So, Aman chosed Butler’s “with date and time” mode and set a location for “bouquet parlour” which comes in between his way back.

Solution Analysis: Because of the availability of choose mode feature between “only location” and “with date and time” , limitations of user’s choice is radically decreased and it has amplified the engagement chances.

Validation and Feedback

Butler being a location based task reminder app, its architecture goes beyond the basic limitations of any task reminder app. After user testing, analyzing attributes such as individual location set (without date and time constraints) and vice versa is a vital aspect for its real time validation.

Furthermore, after then final handoff Butler is considered optimal in UX/UI phase and development phase can be carried out smoothly.
